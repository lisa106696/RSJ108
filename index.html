Lisa Cervantes's "How to Set Up 3-Point Lighting"

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to make your lighting setup look more professional.

Time Needed: 40 minutes (or longer)
Can apply to: Interviews, Film, Photo Shoots, etc.

Want to make your video's look more artistic and high-budget? Taking a few minutes of careful preparation with your lighting setup will make any project of yours go from 0 to 100!

  • Things you'll need to practice:

  • A 3-point lighting kit (or any 3 sources of light)
  • A subject
  • Camera
  • Tripod
  • Multiple power strips/extension chords
  • Transportable Background (optional)
  • Lighting Gels (optional)

  1. Plan the location of where you want to film!

    It's important to plan beforehand a good location for what your filming. A good location consists of:

    -Accessible power outlets

    -Controlled main lighting (outside is tricker for lighting because of the Sun)

    -Lots of space

  2. Rent out lighting equipment, or improvise!

    Quality lighting kits can be very expensive, so it's reasonable if you don't want to buy one for yourself! If you're a university student or have access to a local library, you can rent out 3-point lighting kits, LED panels, reflectors, or anything that would help out with your shoot. Or if you have any kinds of lights at home, use them! White surfaces also bounce light, so those are an option too!

  3. Once you have your equipment, set it up and practice on location with your subject!

    Here are the main lights in this set up:

    -Key Light

    -Fill Light

    -Back Light

  4. Set up your Key Light!

    Your Key light is the main source of lighting. This will be positioned towards the left or right side, and the closest distance from your subject.

  5. Set up your Fill Light!

    Your Fill light is the secondary source of lighting. This will be positioned towards the opposite front side of the Key light, and be farther away from the subject to fill in any shadows the Key light is creating.

  6. Set up your Back Light!

    Your Back light is the additional source of light. This will be positioned towards the back of the subject angled upwards, and create an outline along the subject for more dimensions.
